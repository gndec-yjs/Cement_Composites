Appendix B: Demonstration of Analytical Model Accuracy Using an Illustrative Case

This appendix presents a detailed, step-by-step calculation to demonstrate the accuracy and applicability of the proposed analytical model for predicting the ultimate load-carrying capacity of Steel Fibre Reinforced Concrete (SFRC) beams. The case under consideration corresponds to Set-I, where the volume fraction of steel fibres (\( V_f \)) is 0.75%, and the exposure duration is 1 month. The experimental result for this case is used for comparison with the calculated value obtained from the analytical model.

1. Analytical Model Expression

The general expression for the moment coefficient derived from the proposed analytical model is given by:

$$ \frac{M_u}{f_{ck} B D^2} = 0.24\xi \left( \frac{h_1}{D} \right)^2 + \frac{\beta}{2} \left( \frac{h_2}{D} \right)^2 - \frac{\beta}{2} \frac{h_2}{D} \frac{m}{D} + \frac{\beta}{6} \left( \frac{m}{D} \right)^2 + f_l \gamma \omega \frac{h_2}{D} \left( 1 - \frac{d'}{D} \frac{D}{h_2} \right) $$

Where:

  • \( \xi \) = 1 (for SFRC)
  • \( \beta \) = Fibre index = 0.045 (for \( V_f = 0.75\% \))
  • \( f_l \) = Metal loss factor = \( 1 - \eta = 1 - 0.6975 = 0.3025 \)
  • \( \gamma \) = Stress mobilization factor = 0.87
  • \( \omega \) = Reinforcement index = 0.314
  • \( m/D \) = Depth affected by corrosion = 0.4
  • \( d'/D \) = 0.25
  • \( f_{ck} \) = 25 MPa, \( B \) = 100 mm, \( D \) = 100 mm
  • Length \( L \) = 450 mm

2. Calculation of Neutral Axis Coefficients

To begin, we calculate the neutral axis coefficients \( \frac{h_1}{D} \) and \( \frac{h_2}{D} \):

$$ \frac{h_1}{D} = 2.4 \times \frac{\beta \left( 1 - \frac{m}{2D} \right) + f_l \gamma \omega}{\xi + 2.4 \beta} $$

Substituting the values:

$$ \frac{h_1}{D} = 2.4 \times \frac{0.045 \left( 1 - \frac{0.4}{2} \right) + 0.3025 \times 0.87 \times 0.314}{1 + 2.4 \times 0.045} = 0.257 $$

$$ \frac{h_2}{D} = 1 - \frac{h_1}{D} = 1 - 0.257 = 0.743 $$

3. Calculation of Moment Coefficient

Next, we compute the moment coefficient \( \frac{M_u}{f_{ck} B D^2} \):

$$ \frac{M_u}{f_{ck} B D^2} = 0.24 \times 1 \times (0.257)^2 + \frac{(0.743)^2 \times 0.045}{2} - \frac{0.045}{2} \times 0.743 \times 0.4 + \frac{0.045}{6} \times (0.4)^2 + 0.3025 \times 0.87 \times 0.314 \times 0.743 \times \left( 1 - \frac{0.25}{0.743} \right) $$

Simplifying this expression yields:

$$ \frac{M_u}{f_{ck} B D^2} = 0.064 $$

4. Calculation of Ultimate Moment (Mu)

Now, using the value of \( \frac{M_u}{f_{ck} B D^2} \), we can calculate the ultimate moment \( M_u \) in \( N \cdot mm \):

$$ M_u = 0.064 \times 25 \times 100 \times 100^2 = 1,588,136.93 \, N \cdot mm $$

5. Calculation of Ultimate Load (Pu)

Finally, we calculate the ultimate load \( P_u \) in kN for the case of three-point bending, with a point load applied at the center of the beam:

$$ P_u = \frac{4 \times M_u}{L} = \frac{4 \times 1,588,136.93}{450} = 14.12 \, kN $$

6. Comparison with Experimental Results

The calculated value of the ultimate load \( P_u \) is 14.12 kN. The experimentally observed value for this case was 14.40 kN. Thus, the predicted load from the analytical model shows a close match with the experimental value, confirming the accuracy and applicability of the model.

7. Conclusion

The step-by-step calculation provided here demonstrates the accuracy of the proposed analytical model for predicting the ultimate load-carrying capacity of SFRC beams. The comparison with experimental results shows that the model is highly applicable for real-life scenarios.
